# A Predictive Analysis of Spatial Friction and Non-Linear Contextual Interactions on In-Vehicle Mobile Marketing Coupon Acceptance Rates

**Author:** A G Sanduwara  
**Course:** CIS-3032  

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project investigates the predictive factors behind in-vehicle mobile marketing coupon acceptance rates, with a particular focus on **spatial friction** and **non-linear contextual interactions**. By leveraging machine learning and statistical modeling techniques, this study aims to uncover how location-based variables, driving context, and environmental conditions jointly influence a driver's or passenger's likelihood of accepting a digital coupon delivered via mobile marketing.

---

## 🎯 Research Objectives

- Analyze the role of **spatial friction** (distance, detour cost, route deviation) in coupon acceptance decisions
- Explore **non-linear contextual interactions** between variables such as time of day, weather, destination type, and passenger status
- Build and evaluate predictive models to classify coupon acceptance behavior
- Identify key features that drive acceptance rates in in-vehicle mobile marketing scenarios

## 📊 Dataset

The dataset includes contextual and behavioral variables related to in-vehicle mobile coupon delivery scenarios, such as:

- Coupon type and expiration
- Distance to coupon destination
- Weather and time conditions
- Passenger and destination context
- Driver behavior and acceptance outcome

---

## 🔬 Methodology

1. **Data Preprocessing** — Handling missing values, encoding categorical variables, feature scaling
2. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** — Understanding distributions, correlations, and patterns
3. **Feature Engineering** — Constructing spatial friction metrics and interaction terms
4. **Model Development** — Training classification models (Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, Random Forest, etc.)
5. **Evaluation** — Accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, and ROC-AUC analysis
6. **Interpretation** — Feature importance and insights into acceptance behavior

---

## 📈 Expected Outcomes

- A trained predictive model capable of classifying coupon acceptance with high accuracy
- Insights into the most influential spatial and contextual factors
- Recommendations for improving mobile marketing targeting strategies in vehicular contexts
